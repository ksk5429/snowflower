# Title formulas v2 (Pain Point SEO)

**Replaces v1.** v2 incorporates Pain Point SEO research (Grow & Convert): bottom-of-funnel queries convert 5-7% vs <0.5% for top-of-funnel. Same effort, ~10x revenue.

## Three pain-point patterns that win (use these by default)

### Pattern 1: Comparison ("[device] vs [device] for [population]")

The query OTs and SLPs actually google when narrowing demo candidates.

| Title | Target search |
|---|---|
| MouthPad vs Quha Zono 2 vs Glassouse — which one for C2-C4 SCI? | "mouthpad vs quha" + "best hands-free input C2 SCI" |
| Tobii PCEye vs MouthPad — eye-gaze vs intra-oral, decision tree | "tobii vs mouthpad", "eye gaze vs mouth control" |
| AssistiveTouch vs Switch Control vs head mouse — what each one is actually for | "assistivetouch vs switch control" |
| Quha Zono 2 vs SmartNav 4 — head-mouse comparison after the SmartNav re-launch | "quha vs smartnav" |

### Pattern 2: Funding-path ("How to get [device] covered by [funding path]")

The single most underserved query in the AT space. Tobii Dynavox built a whole funding-navigation team because this is the friction.

| Title | Target search |
|---|---|
| How to get a MouthPad covered by Medicaid waiver in California (2026) | "mouthpad medicaid", "medicaid SGD waiver" |
| How to get a Tobii eye tracker through the VA in 2026 | "VA eye tracker", "VA assistive technology benefit" |
| How to get assistive tech in your IEP — step by step | "IEP assistive technology", "AT in IEP" |
| 보조공학기기 KEAD 신청 가이드 (MouthPad / Tobii / Quha 사례) | "KEAD 보조공학기기 지원 신청" |
| What private insurance actually covers for AAC devices in 2026 | "insurance AAC coverage" |

### Pattern 3: Role-specific guide ("[role] guide to [category]")

| Title | Target |
|---|---|
| The OT's guide to hands-free input devices in 2026 | OT specialist |
| The SLP's guide to choosing between MouthPad, eye-gaze, and AAC switches | SLP gatekeeper |
| The AT specialist's guide to evaluating MouthPad for school IEPs | School-district AT specialist |
| The VA prosthetist's guide to high-cervical-SCI input devices | VA path |
| The caregiver's guide to first-month MouthPad adjustment | Family / caregiver |

## LinkedIn hook templates (≤200 chars before truncate)

LinkedIn truncates at ~210 chars. Hook wins or loses reach in that window.

| Hook type | Template | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Contrarian statement | "Most people think X. The data says Y." | "Most accessibility marketing leads with what the device cures. The data says buyers care about what it lets them do today." |
| Statistic-first | "[number] [unit] of [thing] — and almost no one talks about it" | "$87B of unspent IEP-AT funds in 2024. Almost no one talks about why." |
| Problem framing | "Why [obvious thing] is actually [unexpected thing]" | "Why a $5K MouthPad is cheaper than a $0 'free trial.'" |
| Behind-the-scenes | "I just spent [time] on [task]. Here's what surprised me." | "I just spent 3 hours benchmarking the MouthPad against a Tobii PCEye. The latency story is not what you'd guess." |

Reply rate ≥80% within first 60 minutes is the algorithmic threshold.

## YouTube title + thumbnail co-design rules

- Title and thumbnail are tested as a pair, not separately.
- One face / one object / one question — readable at 10% zoom or kill an element.
- Avoid: "I tried…", "You won't believe…", any word ending in "!"
- Lead with the conclusion or the stakes, not the topic.

| Topic | Weak | Strong |
|---|---|---|
| MouthPad review | "MouthPad Review!" | "MouthPad: 18 months later" |
| Comparison | "Which is best?" | "We tested every hands-free mouse for 30 days" |
| Funding | "Insurance and AAC" | "How Maria got her Tobii covered (the actual paperwork)" |

## Anti-patterns (ban list — auto-reject in script review)

- Inspiration porn: "brave," "didn't let it stop them," "your excuse is invalid"
- Cure framing: "this device cures / fixes / overcomes [condition]"
- Pity framing: slow piano, somber voiceover, before/after as transformation
- Ableist metaphors: "turn a blind eye," "fall on deaf ears," "tone-deaf," "lame"
- Outdated terminology: "wheelchair-bound" → "wheelchair user"; "high/low-functioning" (Autistic community has rejected); "handicapped" → "disabled"
- Unsolicited tragedy backstory ("X used to walk, then the accident happened…") — only include if subject volunteers and approves the cut
- Clickbait that overpromises ("This will change EVERYTHING")
- "Hack" / "secret" / "trick" — undermines AT credibility
